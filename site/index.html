What it shows you

Anchored to the exact five_hour / seven_day percentages Claude reports — the same numbers /usage shows.

Directional standing

The session rail fills green from the left when you're banking a cushion, red from the right when you're over your sustainable pace. Longer red = deeper hole.

🔢 Numbers that roll

Every figure counts one digit at a time like an odometer — never a jarring jump — with a trailing ↑ / ↓ k/min that tells you if you're catching up or falling behind right now.

⛽ Weekly fuel tank

A second rail for the seven-day wall, with an even-burn pace tick. Maxing your 5-hour cap every window drains the week early — maxx paces you so it lasts.

🔒 Nothing leaves your box

No analytics, no third party, no phone-home. All parsing of your Claude Code usage happens entirely on your own machine.

How to read the bar

Two rails, always on.

session     ·

 

week     ▐▌  234,978k left · −102,274k over · 5d
  • session — your rolling 5-hour standing. Green = banked, red = over. The number is your signed cushion; the arrow is your live recovery/loss rate.
  • week — your weekly reserve as a fuel tank: fill is budget remaining, the tick marks even-burn pace, then tokens left and days to reset.
  • context — a meta line shows how full the model context is (separate from the account limit), plus model, branch, spend and cache-hit rate.

Install

One pasteable line in your terminal. Restart Claude Code — done.

$ curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/goodindustries/Maxx/main/tokenmaxx/install.sh | bash

Prefer the plugin manager (skill only, no bar)? In Claude Code: /plugin marketplace add goodindustries/Maxx then /plugin install maxx@maxx.

Then type /maxx any time for total tokens, tokens/day, cache-hit rate and streak — or /maxx session for how much to burn this window.

Your stuff stays yours

maxx has no analytics service and sends no data anywhere. Fully on-box, zero egress. Open source under MIT.
